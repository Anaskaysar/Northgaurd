[OPEN]
Good [morning/afternoon]. We're NorthMind. We built Northern Shift Guard — explainable AI safety screening for Northern Ontario mining shift starts.

[PROBLEM — slide 2]
Mines here are losing experienced workers faster than they can replace them. At shift start, supervisors check PPE and watch for fatigue — but it's manual, inconsistent, and usually not recorded.

If something goes wrong later — injury, inspection, dispute — there's often no record of what was seen or why a worker was allowed on the floor. That's our problem, and it's squarely in the Mining track.

[SOLUTION — slide 3]
Our tool matches real supervisor workflow. Select the mine zone — surface, open pit, ramp, stope, plant. Each zone has different PPE rules under Ontario Reg 854. Upload a photo. You get: what was detected, zone compliance per item, a prioritized supervisor action, and a stored audit record.

[DEMO — live or slide 5]
Step 1: Zone = Active Stope. Upload worker missing hard hat.
→ Required, not detected, non-compliant. Stop-work before underground entry.

Step 2 — KEY MOMENT: Same photo. Switch to Surface / Yard.
→ Hard hat not required. Verdict changes. Generic detectors false-alarm here.

Step 3: Compliant worker in Open Pit → green pass.

Step 4: Fatigue case → monitor action, not auto-removal. Screening aid only, not diagnosis.

[AUDIT — slide 6]
Every scan is logged: zone, evidence, compliance, action, timestamp. Explainable for supervisors and regulators — not one opaque score.

We don't replace supervisors. Fatigue isn't medical diagnosis. Bad lighting affects vision. Production needs offline fallback — we scoped the hackathon to prove the decision pipeline.

[CLOSE]
Impact: faster, consistent, documented shift-start screening tied to zone-specific regulation.

Next: webcam at cage entry, batch crew scanning, more PPE types, compliance trends.

Northern Shift Guard isn't "helmet yes/no." It's: what was seen → what this zone requires → what to do now → why — with a record.

Thank you.

Q&A — PROBABLE QUESTIONS & ANSWERS
═══════════════════════════════════════

Q: How is this different from regular object detection?

A: Object detection asks "Is there a hard hat?"
   We ask: "For THIS zone, can this worker proceed, what should the supervisor do, and why — with a record?"

   Three layers beyond detection:
   1. Zone compliance — same photo, different rules
   2. Nemotron reasoning — prioritized action, not just labels
   3. Audit log — traceable for regulated environments

   Detection is step one, not the product.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: Why GPT-4o vision instead of YOLO on the edge?

A: YOLO is fast for boxes. Mines need plain-language evidence, fatigue cues, and reasoning supervisors trust.

   Architecture is config-driven so YOLO could swap in later. Hackathon proves explainability + zone logic, not cage-side FPS.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: Can fatigue detection work? Wrong calls are dangerous.

A: We don't auto-ban on fatigue. We flag monitor/escalate. Screening aid only. Supervisor stays decision-maker. Production pairs with fit-for-duty protocols.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: Privacy — storing worker photos?

A: Shift-start checks already happen visually; we make them consistent and logged.

   Production needs: retention policy, role-based access, optional on-device processing, union/consent agreements. Not open-ended site surveillance.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: Bad lighting underground?

A: Vision struggles in low light — we show evidence and uncertainty, not fake confidence. Production uses controlled checkpoint lighting + human override when low confidence.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: Does this replace supervisors?

A: No. Supports them — faster checks, consistent zone rules, documented reasoning. Nemotron output is recommendation, not automatic gate.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: False positives stop production.

A: Zone rules prevent that. Global "no hard hat" would block surface workers. We tie alerts to zone requirements.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: What if API goes down at shift change?

A: Valid concern. MVP proves pipeline. Production needs queued offline mode, cached zone rules, optional local vision. Won't ship cloud-only without fallback.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: API cost at scale?

A: Shift-start is batch at cage time — hundreds of photos per shift, not 24/7 video. Cost manageable vs incident cost. Batching/analytics on roadmap.

───────────────────────────────────────

Q: What's actually original?

A: Not "we detect PPE." It's zone-aware compliance + reasoning + audit for Ontario mining. Demo proof: same image, different zone, different verdict.

Northern Shift Guard helps mining supervisors at shift start. Pick the zone, upload a photo, get zone-specific PPE compliance, a clear supervisor action, and an audit record.

Same worker photo can pass on surface and fail in active stope — because rules change by zone. That's what separates us from a regular detector.

We built it end to end: FastAPI, React, GPT-4o vision, Nemotron reasoning, audit storage. Fatigue is screening only. Humans stay in charge.

Impact: documented, explainable safety decisions instead of black-box glances. Thank you.
